## Reflection

The claim that looked most authoritative but was actually weakest was the VIT Vellore placement rate claim. Gemini presented the statistic confidently and associated it with a recognized institution, making it appear highly credible at first glance. Perplexity also surfaced supporting information during the verification step. However, when I reviewed the primary source more carefully, I found that the placement percentage was not an exact match to the original claim because the context and eligibility criteria differed. This demonstrated that even when a statistic appears to be correct, the framing, year, or population being measured may not align perfectly with the AI-generated statement.

The key lesson from this exercise is that confidence does not equal correctness. AI tools can generate convincing and highly specific statistics, but those statistics must always be verified against primary sources before being accepted. Verification is a human responsibility, and opening the original source remains an essential step in evaluating the reliability of AI-generated information.
